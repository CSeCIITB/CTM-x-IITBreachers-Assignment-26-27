# Description

Hello future CTMs!! 
Although the work of a CTM is highly technical, it also involves designing posters, banners, and other promotional material where a sense for asthetics and clarity in communication is important.

This assignment will help guage your level of comfort with basic design tools (we reccomend canva but you can use any tool you're comfortable with).

While we understand that many of you may not have past experience with design, fear not! Try to focus on communicating the important details and designing something that looks good to you. Think of this as an opportunity to learn. We are excited to see how you approach it.

### Submission Details

Submission can be in any digital format such as PDF, PNG etc. You can use tools like Canva, Figma or any other tool you are comforable with.

For being considered as a CTM applicant it is **Mandatory** to submit at least one of these designs. 

You're welcome—and encouraged—to submit **both**, but submitting at least one is **compulsory** for your application to be considered.



Enough blabbering from our side—let’s dive into the assignments and see what you’ve got!

## 1: 🎨 Design a T-Shirt or Hoodie for the Cybersecurity Club! 🔒👕

This is your chance to leave a visual mark on the Club get creative, and show us your style!, we want to see your take on what a Cybersecurity Club tee/hoodie should look like.

A few ideas to spark your imagination:

    Cool hacker vibes (think: terminal aesthetics, glitch art, retro synth)

    Witty one-liners or puns (We love our bad jokes )

    Visuals involving locks, code, firewalls, viruses, or abstract digital art

    Don't forget to add the CSeC logo which is provided in this directory.

There are no strict rules—just try to ensure the design reflects some flavour of cybersecurity and is something you’d proudly wear. Bonus points for originality and clean composition!




## 2: 🖼️ Design a Poster for “Hacking via CTFs” 🧠💻
Our Freshers' Orientation Event for the Cybersecurity Club

This poster will be the first thing freshers see—so it should be interesting and encourage them to come to the event.
🔍 What to Include:

    Title: Hacking via CTFs

    Tagline (optional): Something like "Your first step into the world of Cybersecurity". Or come up with something of your own (or ask chatgpt ;) )

    Event Description: This is a beginners event with no pre-requisites. It introduces students to CTFs. Keeping this in mind write an appropriate event description

    Date, Time & Venue: (You can make these up for now)

    Organizer and contact details: Who is organizing the event? and who to contact for further details should be clear from the poster.


Let the fun begin. We’re excited to see what you come up with! 🔓💥
